Testing Standards

SAE J2707 Wear Test Procdeure on Inertia Dynamometer

Foreword

The SAE J2707 is a recommended practice widely used around the globe. New testing methods and equipment do not affect the execution of the procedure. The SAE J2707 is intended to harmonize test procedures for simulating and evalulating wear characteristics of automotive brake lining and pads that relate to real world conditons of vehicle on the market.

Objective

Brake wear life, its respective dynamics, and behavior under different temperatures and speeds are critical to the successful development and production of commerically available disc brake pads and linings, nboth for the OM equipment and aftermarket. The evaulation fo the wear cheacteristics is also critical given friction material and its mating part when subjected to a series of brake stops derived from real world driving patterns. Different permutations of speed, temperature, and deceleration forseeable in driving situautions are utilized to yield different levels of wear and transfer later phonomena. When tested under controlled conditions, these driving pattern combinations should provide valubale insight on the wear behavior and level of given lining and rotor or drum combiniation
